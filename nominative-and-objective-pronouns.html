Which pronoun is the hero of the sentence? Let's find out!

Learning Objectives üéØ

  • Understand how pronouns function as the doers and receivers of an action.
  • Differentiate between nominative (subjective/subject) and objective (object) pronouns.
  • Identify subject and object pronouns.
  • Use subject and object pronouns correctly in your own sentences.

The Nominative Squad! ‚ú®

Think of the nominative pronouns as the heroes of a sentence. They are the subject, which means they are the person or thing that is doing the main action. If you can ask "who" or "what" is doing the verb in a sentence, the answer will be a nominative pronoun. For example, in the sentence "She ran to the store," the pronoun "she" is the hero who is doing the running!

Example Sentences:

  • I am the captain of the team.
  • You should read this book.
  • He is a fantastic dancer.
  • She wrote a beautiful poem.
  • It rained all night long.
  • We are going to the concert together.
  • They built a huge sandcastle.
  • My friends and I went to the movies.
  • The dog and it played in the yard.
  • Both of us will pass the test.

I

Use it for yourself.

You

Use it for the person you are talking to.

He/She/It

Use it for one person or thing.

We

Use it for yourself and others.

They

Use it for a group of people or things.

The Objective Team! üèÜ

Objective pronouns are the receivers of the action! They are the object in a sentence. This means the action of the verb is being done to them. You can find them by asking "who" or "what" is receiving the action. For instance, in the sentence "The ball hit him," the pronoun "him" is the one receiving the action of being hit.

Example Sentences:

  • Can you give the paper to me?
  • I told you the secret.
  • She gave the book to him.
  • We saw her at the mall.
  • The dog chased it across the field.
  • The teacher showed the project to us.
  • The principal praised them for their hard work.
  • They gave the gifts to my brother and me.
  • I gave a hug to both of them.
  • The surprise was meant for us.

Me

Use it when the action is for you.

You

Use it when the action is for the other person.

Him/Her/It

Use it for one person or thing.

Us

Use it for you and others.

Them

Use it when the action is for a group of people or things.
