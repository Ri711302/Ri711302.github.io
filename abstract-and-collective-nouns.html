A noun is a naming word. It can be a person, place, thing, or even an idea. Today, we'll become noun detectives and learn about two very special kinds: Abstract Nouns and Collective Nouns. Let's go! üöÄ

Abstract Nouns ‚ú®

Abstract nouns are special because you can't see, touch, hear, smell, or taste them. They are things you can only think about! They are ideas, feelings, or qualities.

For example: You can feel happiness, but you can't hold it. You can see a hero, but you can't touch courage itself. Other examples are: love, friendship, freedom, and honesty.

Collective Nouns ü§ù

Collective nouns are words for a whole group of people, animals, or things. Even though the word is singular, it's talking about many things together!

For example: A flock of birds flew by. The word "flock" means many birds acting as one group! Other fun examples are: a team of players, a herd of elephants, or a bunch of grapes.
