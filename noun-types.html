Common, Proper, Abstract, and Collective Nouns

1. Introduction to Nouns

A noun is the name of a person, animal, place, thing, or idea. Nouns help us identify and talk about people, objects, concepts, or groups.

Examples: teacher, book, Lagos, happiness, family.

There are different types of nouns. In this lesson, we will focus on Common Nouns, Proper Nouns, Abstract Nouns, and Collective Nouns.

2. Common Nouns

Definition: A common noun is a general name for any person, animal, place, or thing. It does not refer to a specific one.

Rule: Common nouns always begin with a small letter, unless they start a sentence.

  • Person: girl, teacher, doctor, boy
  • Place: city, school, river, market
  • Thing: chair, pencil, bag, car
  • Animal: dog, lion, goat, bird

  • The boy is playing football.
  • She bought a new dress.

3. Proper Nouns

Definition: A proper noun is the special name of a particular person, place, or thing.

Rule: Proper nouns always begin with a capital letter.

  • People: Nelson Mandela, Bola Tinubu, Messi
  • Places: Abuja, Nigeria, Atlantic Ocean, Mount Everest
  • Things: Christmas, Monday, Coca-Cola, Bible

âœ” Sentence Examples:

  • Ariana is the class president.
  • We live in Lagos.
  • Mount Kilimanjaro is in Africa.

4. Abstract Nouns

Definition: An abstract noun is the name of a quality, idea, feeling, or state that cannot be seen or touched. It only exists in the mind.

Rule: Abstract nouns are not physical; we cannot see, touch, hear, or taste them.

  • Qualities: honesty, kindness, bravery, intelligence
  • Feelings: love, anger, joy, fear, sadness
  • States/Ideas: freedom, poverty, wisdom, education, peace

âœ” Sentence Examples:

  • Children should show kindness to others.
  • The people are fighting for freedom.
  • Her happiness is important to me.

5. Collective Nouns

Definition: A collective noun is the name of a group of people, animals, or things considered as one unit.

Rule: Collective nouns may look singular but can represent many members in a group.

  • People: team, committee, class, family, crowd
  • Animals: flock (of birds), herd (of cattle), pride (of lions), swarm (of bees)
  • Things: bunch (of keys), library (of books), fleet (of ships), bouquet (of flowers)

âœ” Sentence Examples:

  • The team won the football match.
  • A flock of birds flew across the sky.
  • The committee will meet tomorrow.

7. Fun Facts

  • The word "noun" comes from the Latin word nomen, meaning "name".
  • Some collective nouns have very unusual names, e.g., a murder of crows or a parliament of owls.
