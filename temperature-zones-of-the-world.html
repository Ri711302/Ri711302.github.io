What are Temperature Zones? ü§î

The Earth is a giant sphere, and the Sun's rays don't hit every part of it the same way. This is why some places are very hot all year, while others have cold winters and others are cold all the time! We can divide the Earth into three main temperature zones based on how much sunlight they receive. Let's explore these fascinating zones!

The Torrid Zone üî•

The Torrid Zone (also known as the Tropical Zone) is the hottest zone on Earth! It is located between the Tropic of Cancer (23.5¬∞N) and the Tropic of Capricorn (23.5¬∞S). The Sun's rays hit this area directly, making it very hot and humid all year.

Countries in the Torrid Zone often experience:

  • Hot and humid weather with no distinct winter.
  • High rainfall and tropical forests.
  • This zone includes countries like Nigeria, Brazil, and Indonesia.

The Temperate Zone üçÇ

The Temperate Zone has four distinct seasons: spring, summer, autumn, and winter. This is because the Sun's rays hit these regions at an angle, which changes throughout the year. The Northern Temperate Zone is located between the Tropic of Cancer and the Arctic Circle, while the Southern Temperate Zone is between the Tropic of Capricorn and the Antarctic Circle.

Life in the Temperate Zone often involves:

  • Warm summers and cool or cold winters.
  • Beautiful changes in nature with each season.
  • This zone includes countries like the United States, most of Europe, and Argentina.

The Frigid Zone ‚ùÑÔ∏è

The Frigid Zone (also known as the Polar Zone) is the coldest part of the world! It is located from the Arctic Circle to the North Pole and from the Antarctic Circle to the South Pole. Here, the Sun's rays hit at a very low angle, or not at all for parts of the year, leading to extremely cold conditions.

Characteristics of the Frigid Zone include:

  • Winters with very long nights and freezing temperatures.
  • Summers with long daylight hours but still very cold.
  • This zone includes countries like parts of Canada, Russia, and the entire continent of Antarctica.
