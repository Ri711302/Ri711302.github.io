Pronouns and Their Types

1. What is a Pronoun?

A pronoun is a word that is used in place of a noun to avoid unnecessary repetition in speech or writing. Instead of repeating the same noun, we use a pronoun to make sentences smoother and clearer.

Example:
Rita is my friend. Rita is very kind. (Repetition of “Rita”)
Rita is my friend. She is very kind. (The pronoun she replaces “Rita”).

Functions of Pronouns:

  • Avoid repetition
  • Make sentences clearer
  • Show possession
  • Connect clauses
  • Emphasise meaning

2. Types of Pronouns

A. Personal Pronouns

These refer to specific people or things. They change depending on person (first, second, third), number (singular, plural), and case (subject, object).

Examples:

  • Subjective case (doer): I, we, you, he, she, it, they
  • Objective case (receiver): me, us, you, him, her, it, them
Example sentences:
I am a teacher.
Can you help me with this work?
This pen is mine.
They are good friends.
That car belongs to him.

B. Reflexive Pronouns

These are used when the subject and object of the verb are the same person/thing. They end in -self (singular) or -selves (plural).

Examples: myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, yourselves, themselves

Example sentences:
I looked at myself in the mirror.
She taught herself French.
The cat cleaned itself.
We enjoyed ourselves at the park.
The boys punished themselves for the mistake.

C. Demonstrative Pronouns

Used to point out specific people or things.

Examples: this, that, these, those

Example sentences:
This is my book.
That is your bag.
These are our chairs.
Those are beautiful flowers.

D. Interrogative Pronouns

Used to ask questions.

Examples: who, whom, whose, which, what

Example sentences:
Who is at the door?
To whom did you give the note?
Whose bag is this?
Which of the pencils is yours?
What are you writing?

E. Relative Pronouns

Used to join clauses and refer back to a noun mentioned earlier.

Examples: who, whom, whose, which, that

Example sentences:
The man who is talking is my uncle.
The boy whom you saw is my cousin.
The woman whose car was stolen is crying.
The pen which you bought is blue.
The story that he told us is true.

F. Indefinite Pronouns

Refer to people or things in a general or unspecific way.

Examples: someone, anyone, nobody, everybody, each, all, few, several, many, some, none, anybody, everything, nothing

Example sentences:
Someone is waiting outside.
Anybody can take part in the game.
Nobody was absent today.
Each of the pupils has a book.
Many are called, but few are chosen.
Nothing is impossible.
All of us were excited.

G. Possessive Pronouns

Show ownership or belonging.

Examples: mine, yours, his, hers, its, ours, theirs

Example sentences:
This book is mine.
That pencil is yours.
The red dress is hers.
The puppy wagged its tail.
That house is ours.
The shoes are theirs.

H. Reciprocal Pronouns

Show a mutual relationship or action between two or more people.

Examples: each other, one another

Example sentences:
The two friends hugged each other.
We should love one another.
The classmates congratulated each other after the exam.

I. Emphatic Pronouns

These are the same as reflexive pronouns but are used for emphasis.

Examples: myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, yourselves, themselves

Example sentences:
I myself cleaned the classroom.
The teacher herself praised the pupils.
The principal himself gave the speech.
We ourselves solved the problem.
They themselves built the hut.

4. Summary

A pronoun replaces a noun to avoid repetition.

There are different types of pronouns, each with its own function.

Pronouns make communication clearer, shorter, and more meaningful.
