The Solar System

A clear, structured note covering meaning, the Sun, planets, dwarf planets, moons, small bodies, importance, and a concise summary.

1. Meaning of the Solar System

The Solar System is the collection of the Sun, eight planets, their moons, and many smaller objects such as asteroids, meteoroids, and comets that move around the Sun. The word “solar” comes from Sol, meaning Sun. The Solar System is held together by the Sun’s gravitational pull.

2. The Sun

  • The Sun is a star and the center of the Solar System.
  • It provides light and heat, making life on Earth possible.
  • Mainly made of hydrogen and helium.
  • It is so large that over one million Earths could fit inside it (by volume).

3. The Planets

The eight planets are grouped into inner rocky planets and outer gas/ice giants.

(a) Inner Planets (Rocky)

  1. Mercury – Closest to the Sun; very hot in the day, very cold at night.
  2. Venus – Hottest planet due to thick atmosphere; bright in the sky.
  3. Earth – Our home; has liquid water and life-supporting air.
  4. Mars – The “Red Planet”; dusty deserts and signs of ancient water.

(b) Outer Planets (Gas/Ice Giants)

  1. Jupiter – Largest planet; has a Great Red Spot (a giant storm).
  2. Saturn – Famous for its beautiful rings of ice and rock.
  3. Uranus – Rotates on its side; blue-green colour from methane gas.
  4. Neptune – Farthest planet; very cold; strongest winds.

4. Dwarf Planets

Dwarf planets are smaller bodies that orbit the Sun and are roughly spherical, but they have not cleared other objects from their orbits.

  • Pluto – The best known dwarf planet (once considered the ninth planet).
  • Ceres – Located in the Asteroid Belt.
  • Haumea, Makemake, and Eris – Found in the distant Kuiper Belt.

5. Moons

  • Moons are natural satellites that orbit planets.
  • Earth has one moon.
  • Jupiter and Saturn each have many dozens of moons.
  • Some moons (e.g., Jupiter’s Europa) may have subsurface oceans.

6. Other Bodies in the Solar System

  • Asteroids – Rocky objects, mostly in the Asteroid Belt between Mars and Jupiter.
  • Comets – Icy bodies that develop glowing tails when near the Sun.
  • Meteoroids – Small rocks in space; when they enter Earth’s atmosphere they become meteors (shooting stars), and if they reach the ground they are meteorites.

7. Importance of the Solar System

  • The Sun provides energy for life on Earth.
  • Studying planets and moons helps us understand how worlds form.
  • Asteroids and comets preserve clues about the early Solar System.
  • Exploration inspires technology, science learning, and possible future space travel.

8. Summary

  • The Solar System includes the Sun, eight planets, dwarf planets, moons, asteroids, comets, and meteoroids.
  • The Sun’s gravity holds everything together and provides light and heat.
  • Planets are grouped into inner rocky planets and outer gas/ice giants; Earth is unique for supporting life.
