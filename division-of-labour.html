What is Division of Labour?

Division of labour means breaking down a big job into smaller tasks and giving each task to a different person. This way, everyone does a part of the work, and together, the job gets done faster and better. Let's see how a team makes a delicious pizza! 🍕

Advantages of Division of Labour

When we divide the work, amazing things happen!

⚡

Work is Faster

The whole pizza is made much quicker than if one person did everything.

✨

Better Quality

Each person becomes an expert at their job, making the pizza taste even better!

🚀

More is Made

The team can make many more pizzas in the same amount of time.

🎓

You Become an Expert

By doing the same job, you get very good and very fast at it!

Disadvantages of Division of Labour

Sometimes, there can be a few small problems with this type of work.

बोर

It Can Be Boring

Doing the same small job over and over again can get a little boring.

😵‍💫

Lack of Skills

You only learn one part of the job, so you might not know how to make a whole pizza yourself.

🤖

Losing Your Job

If your job is very simple, a machine might be able to do it one day, which could take your job.

Where do we see Division of Labour?

At home 🏠

Cooking, cleaning, shopping.

In schools 🏫

Teachers, principal, helpers, drivers, security officers, cleaners.

In factories 🏭

Different workers for different jobs, drivers, security officers, cleaners.

In banks 🏦

Customer service staff, account officer, manager, loan officer, security officers, cleaners.

Conclusion

Division of labour helps people work together, finish tasks faster, and do a better job!
