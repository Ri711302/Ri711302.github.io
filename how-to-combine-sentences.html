Relative Pronouns: The Story Detail Makers! üìñ

Relative pronouns like who, whom, whose, which, that, where, when add extra details to make your sentences more interesting. They connect ideas about people, places, or things.

Simple Sentences: The puppy is cute. The puppy chews toys.

Combined: The puppy that chews toys is cute. üê∂

Simple Sentences: My teacher is kind. My teacher helps me read.

Combined: My teacher, who helps me read, is kind. üìö

Simple Sentences: The park is big. We play soccer there.

Combined: The park where we play soccer is big. ‚öΩ

Quick Guide:

  • Who/Whom: For people (e.g., The girl who sings is talented.)
  • Which: For things or animals (e.g., The book which I read was fun.)
  • That: For people, things, or animals (e.g., The car that is red is fast.)
  • Where/When: For places or times (e.g., The day when we met was sunny.)
Fun Fact: Relative pronouns act like detectives, finding and linking details in your story!
