Verbal Reasoning

Letter Series

Ndudi Rita Enemuwe

A letter series is a sequence of letters following a specific pattern or rule. Your job is to find the missing term in the series.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this presentation, you will be able to:

  • • Understand the concept of letter series.
  • • Identify common patterns in a series.
  • • Apply different reasoning methods to solve problems.
  • • Confidently solve various letter series questions.

Common Patterns - Part 1

Most letter series problems are based on one of these simple rules:

  • Alphabetical Order: The series follows the standard A-Z order, either forward or backward.

    Example: A, B, C, D, ?

  • Skipping Letters: The series skips a fixed number of letters between each term.

    Example: A, C, E, G, ? (skipping one letter each time)

  • Positional Value: The pattern is based on the letters' position in the alphabet (A=1, B=2, C=3, etc.).

    Example: B, D, F, H, ? (positions 2, 4, 6, 8)

Common Patterns - Part 2

Here are a few more complex patterns to look out for:

  • Reversed Pairs: The series pairs letters from the beginning of the alphabet with their corresponding letters from the end.

    Example: AZ, BY, CX, ?

  • Alternating Series: Two or more independent series are combined into one.

    Example: A, C, B, D, C, E, ?

Example Problem 1

A, C, E, G, ?

The pattern is to skip one letter each time.

A -> B (skipped) -> C -> D (skipped) -> E -> F (skipped) -> G -> H (skipped) -> I

The answer is I.

Example Problem 2

AZ, BY, CX, ?

The pattern uses reversed pairs. The first letter moves forward alphabetically, and the second letter moves backward.

  • • The first letter of each pair is A, B, C... so the next one is D.
  • • The second letter is Z, Y, X... so the next one is W.

The answer is DW.
