What is the Solar System? üåå

Our Solar System is like our cosmic home! It's made up of the Sun and everything that travels around it, including planets, dwarf planets, moons, asteroids, and comets. It's all held together by the Sun's powerful gravity!

Meet the Planets

Inner Planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars

  • These are closest to the Sun
  • They are small and rocky

Outer Planets: Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune

  • These are farther from the Sun
  • They are big and made mostly of gas

Beyond the Planets ‚ú®

Our Solar System is also home to many other fascinating objects:

  • Dwarf Planets: Like Pluto, Ceres, and Eris. They are round like planets but share their orbits with other objects.
  • Asteroids: Rocky, airless worlds that orbit the Sun, mostly found in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter.
  • Comets: Icy bodies that release gas and dust, forming a "tail" as they get close to the Sun.
  • Moons: Natural satellites that orbit planets (Earth has one, Jupiter has many!).

Why is the Solar System Important? ‚≠ê

  • The Solar System helps us understand our place in the universe.
  • The Sun gives us energy for plants, animals, and people.
  • Studying planets and satellites teaches us about weather, seasons, and even helps us with technology like GPS.
