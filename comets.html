Comets üí´

Comets are often called "dirty snowballs" because they're made of ice, dust, and rock. They orbit the sun, and when they get close, the sun's heat melts the ice, creating a long, bright tail that we can sometimes see from Earth!

Asteroids are big, rocky space rocks! They are much larger than meteoroids and most of them live in a special place between Mars and Jupiter called the Asteroid Belt.

Meteoroids, Meteors, Meteorites üå†

These three names are all for the same thing, but in different locations!

  • A Meteoroid is a small piece of rock or debris floating in space.
  • A Meteor is the flash of light we see when a meteoroid enters Earth's atmosphere and burns up. We often call them "shooting stars!"
  • A Meteorite is the part of the meteoroid that survives its trip through the atmosphere and lands on Earth's surface.
