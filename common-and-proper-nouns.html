Common vs. Proper: A Guide

What are Nouns? 🤔

Nouns are words that name a person, place, thing, or idea. They are the building blocks of sentences! But did you know there are different types? Let's explore two main kinds: Common and Proper Nouns!

Common Nouns 🌍

A common noun names any general person, place, thing, or idea. They are not capitalized unless they start a sentence. Think of them as general categories!

👩‍🏫

teacher

🏫

school

🐶

dog

📚

book

🏞️

park

💡

idea

Proper Nouns 🌟

A proper noun names a specific person, place, thing, or idea. They are always capitalized, no matter where they appear in a sentence!

👩‍🎤

Ms. Smith

🏛️

Google Headquarters

🐶

Fido

📖

The Great Gatsby

🌳

Central Park

🎉

Christmas

Key Differences 🔑

Feature Common Noun Proper Noun
What it names General person, place, thing, or idea Specific person, place, thing, or idea
Capitalization Not capitalized (unless at start of sentence) Always capitalized
Examples city, girl, holiday, car London, Sarah, Christmas, Toyota
