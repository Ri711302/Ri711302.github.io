What Are Parts of Speech?

Parts of speech are categories for words based on their function in a sentence. They are the fundamental building blocks of language, helping us understand how words work together to create meaning.

Noun

A word that represents a person, place, thing, or idea.

Examples:

  • Person: teacher, Mary
  • Place: city, Paris
  • Thing: table, book
  • Idea: freedom, happiness

Verb

A word that describes an action, state, or occurrence.

Examples:

  • Action: run, eat, sing
  • State of Being: is, am, are, was

Adjective

A word that describes or modifies a noun or pronoun.

Examples:

  • tall building
  • red car
  • happy child

Adverb

A word that describes or modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb. Often ends in -ly.

Examples:

  • ran quickly
  • very happy
  • sings loudly

Pronoun

A word that takes the place of a noun.

Examples:

  • He, she, it
  • They, we
  • You, I

Conjunction

A word that connects words, phrases, or clauses.

Examples:

  • and, but, or
  • for, so, yet

Preposition

A word that shows the relationship of a noun or pronoun to another word in the sentence.

Examples:

  • in the box
  • on the table
  • under the bridge
