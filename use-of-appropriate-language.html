Creative Writing

The Power of Language and Vocabulary

By Ndudi Rita Enemuwe

Learn how to bring your stories to life by choosing the perfect words.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this presentation, you will be able to:

  • • Differentiate between generic and specific vocabulary.
  • • Use adjectives and adverbs effectively to "show, not tell."
  • • Employ connectives to create smooth transitions.
  • • Write more descriptive and engaging prose.

Showing, Not Telling

Instead of simply stating a fact, a great writer uses descriptive language to paint a picture in the reader's mind.

Telling

The character was sad.

Showing

Tears welled in her eyes, a single, salty drop tracing a path down her cold cheek.

The Power of Adjectives

Adjectives are words that describe nouns. They add detail and color to your writing, making your descriptions more vivid.

  • Generic: The dog barked.

    Specific: The ferocious dog barked with a deep, rumbling growl.

  • Generic: The house was big.

    Specific: The sprawling, gothic house stood on a lonely hill.

The Use of Adverbs

Adverbs describe verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. They tell you *how*, *when*, *where*, or *to what extent* an action occurs.

Be careful not to overuse adverbs!

  • Better: He ran quickly.

    Even Better: He sprinted. (The verb itself implies the speed)

  • Better: She spoke softly.

    Even Better: She whispered. (The verb itself implies the manner)

Connectives (Transitions)

Connectives are words or phrases that link ideas together and create a smooth flow between sentences and paragraphs.

Examples of Connectives

  • Adding Information: moreover, furthermore, in addition
  • Showing Contrast: however, nevertheless, on the other hand
  • Indicating Cause/Effect: therefore, consequently, as a result
  • Sequencing: first, then, next, finally

Activity 1: Describe It!

Rewrite the following sentences using more specific adjectives and adverbs to make them more descriptive.

  1. The man walked to the store.

    Think about what kind of man, and how he walked.

  2. The girl spoke.

    How did she speak? What was her voice like?

  3. The forest was nice.

    Describe the forest's atmosphere and appearance.

(You can write your answers in a notebook or on a piece of paper.)

Final Challenge

Combine everything you've learned to create a short paragraph based on this simple sentence:

The car drove down the street.

Use vivid adjectives, carefully chosen adverbs, and a connective to make it an engaging piece of writing.

Now, Go Write!

Remember, every word you choose has the power to shape your story. Practice makes perfect.
