The Earth is constantly in motion. These movements are important because they affect day and night, the seasons, climate, and even life on Earth. The two major movements of the Earth are rotation and revolution.

1. Rotation of the Earth

Definition: Rotation is the spinning of the Earth on its imaginary axis.

  • The Earth rotates from west to east (anticlockwise when viewed from the North Pole).
  • Time Taken: One complete rotation takes 24 hours (1 day).

üåû Effects of Rotation:

  • Day and Night: As the Earth spins, the side facing the Sun experiences day, while the opposite side experiences night.
  • Apparent Sunrise and Sunset: The Sun appears to rise in the east and set in the west because of the Earth‚Äôs rotation.
  • Time Differences: Rotation causes different places on Earth to experience different times (time zones).
  • Flattening at the Poles: Due to rotation, the Earth is not a perfect sphere; it is slightly flattened at the poles and bulged at the equator.
